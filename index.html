Local High School Basketball Team Clinches Regional Championship in Thrilling Overtime Victory

SPRINGFIELD, IL - The Riverside High School Panthers secured their first regional basketball championship in a decade last night, defeating the Westfield Wildcats 68-65 in a heart-stopping overtime battle. Senior guard Michael Rodriguez led the Panthers with 22 points, including a clutch three-pointer in the final minute of overtime that sealed the victory. The game was a back-and-forth contest that kept over 2,000 fans on the edge of their seats throughout the evening. "This win means everything to our team and our community," said Panthers head coach David Thompson. "These players have worked incredibly hard all season, and they truly deserved this moment." The Panthers trailed by six points with just three minutes remaining in regulation time before mounting a remarkable comeback. Sophomore center Jason Wong's defensive plays were critical in forcing turnovers that allowed Riverside to tie the game and push it into overtime. Riverside will now advance to the state championships next month, marking the first time in school history that the basketball program has reached this level of competition.
